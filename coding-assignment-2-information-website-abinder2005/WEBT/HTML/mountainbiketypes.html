MTB-Types

Main-Website

 

Overview

As some people might not know there is more than just 1 mountainbike type. There are lots of different bikes for different purposes of usage.
You can distinguish if you wheter like to climb mountains, like to ride down the mountains or like to jump.
On this website you will get to know about the differences between mountainbikes.

 

Cross Country

Usage

The Cross Country or XC bike's main category is climbing mountains. It's made for going up big mountains but not really to ride them down
after you climbed a mountain. Mostly XC-bikers ride down on the street.

Suspension

Most of the XC-bikes are hardtails. That means that they only have a fork in the front. Then there are fullys as well. Fullsuspension
bikes (fullys) do have additional to the fork in the front a rear shok. XC-bikes have approximately 80mm - 120mm of travel. Suspension
is necessary to absorb strong impacts.

Racing

There is a own world cup all about XC-racing. It's called UCI XCO World Cup. The athletes try to get as light weight as possible.
XC-bikes weigh about 8kg - 12kg.

 

All Mountain and Trail bikes

Usage

The all mountain or trailbike is the most common bike. It can be used for simple tours in flat land or for big tours in alpine terrain.
It's good for climbing mountains but you will also have a relatively smooth downhill ride.

Suspension

The usual trailbike is fullsuspensioned with a travel from 120mm - 160mm.

E-Bikes

Since 2015 many manufacturers produce trailbikes with an electric motor. The motor helps you on big tours and supports you until you
reach a velocity of 25km/h. The negative aspect of E-Bikes is the highly increased weight. Normal trailbikes weigh 11kg - 15kg,
whereas E-Trailbikes weigh about 20kg - 25kg.

 

Enduro and Super Enduro

Usage

The endurobike's geometry is designed to ride the bike downhill but also to go up with it. Endurobikes are seen in bikeparks and
mainly in their main discipline. Riding in alpine terrain on high mountains.

Suspension

Enduro bikes have a travel from 150mm - 180mm and are fullsuspensioned. This huge amount of travel allows a comfortable ride down
the mountain. Super enduros or freeride bikes have even more travel and their geometry is more based on riding down a mountain.

Racing

There is a championship called EWS (Enduro World Series) where the best endurobikers race against each other. This couple of very
hard races take place in Italy, Austria, Canada, Switzerland and many more countries.

 

Downhill

Usage

Downhill bikes are designed for the most extreme and roughest terrain. Their geometry is only deigned for descending from
a mountain and not to climb it. You can't climb any mountain with a downhill bike. That's primarly because of their high gear
which they need for pedaling at high speed.

Suspension

Downhill bikes are the bikes with the highest amount of travel on their suspensions. The travel is about 180mm - 215mm and
that gives the bike a relatively stable and comfortable descent even on the rockiest and most root blocked tracks.

Racing

The best and most skilled riders ride in the UCI Downhill Worldcup which takes place all around the world. This year's hosters
were Portugal and Slovenia.

 

Dirtjump/Slopestyle/BMX

Usage

Dirtjump or Slopestyle bikes are bikes for huge jumps. This jumps are primarly built out of earth and boost their riders about
10m - 15m through the air. BMX (Bike Motocross) bikes are ridden in skateparks or indoor skatehalls. BMX bikes are like dirtjump
bikes very light and BMX bikes mostly have no brakes.

Suspension

Dirtjump bikes have a fork with a travel around 120mm. Sometimes they also have a shock in the rear. BMX bikes don't have a
suspension at all.

Competitions

The most important competitions for professional dirtjump riders are Crankworx and Audi Nines. Crankworx takes place in Austria,
New Zealand, Canada, and many other countries. It's not only a dirtjump event but it also features a downhill race. Audi Nines
takes place in Germany.
